# Noah's LinkedIn Post Examples (Ranked by Engagement)

## #1 Most Engaged: pytest-coverage-impact Announcement
Hook strategy: Personal story about AI gaming his test coverage. Problem > discovery > solution arc.
Key pattern: Led with the relatable frustration, showed the journey, named the tool late.
CTA: "Links in comments" with article + GitHub repo.
Hashtags: #Python #SoftwareEngineering #AI #Pytest #OpenSource #DataEngineering

## #2 Most Engaged: Career Search Update
Hook strategy: "I haven't posted an update..." creates curiosity about what changed. Then pivots to gratitude, not job hunting.
Key pattern: Named specific people (Joe Herring, Creed Smith, Morgan Wimmer, etc.). Told micro-stories about each interaction. Made it about relationships, not desperation.
CTA: Ended with "tl;dr - Relationships are king. They take time to build."
Hashtags: #LookingForWork #SnowflakeIsAwesome #LoveBeingADataSuperhero

## #3 Most Engaged: Data Superhero Announcement + Long Game Article
Hook strategy: "I am incredibly honored... But as the excitement settles, I've been reflecting on what that title actually means."
Key pattern: Immediately pivoted from announcement to philosophical question. Used the article content to add substance. Asked a specific engagement question.
CTA: "How do you balance the pressure for 'near-term velocity' with the 'inefficient' work of mentoring the next generation?"
Hashtags: #Snowflake #DataSuperheroes #Leadership #Stewardship #DataEngineering #SnowflakeBuilders #ProfessionalGrowth

## What Works Across All Three
- Personal story before professional announcement
- Specific names, numbers, details (not vague claims)
- The hook lands in the first 2-3 lines before "see more"
- Engagement question that people can answer from their own experience
- "Link in comments" at the bottom (never in body)
